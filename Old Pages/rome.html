Roman Society-I

Published 01 May 2013

" Roman society was the perfect example of a double-edged sword. Roman society would have been very appalling to somebody from another land. The reasons were:

  • Violence as a part of Society
  • Ruthlessness of the Army
  • Abundance of the use of Slaves
  However, we must not forget that Rome was one of the most ingenuity-encouraging civilizations. The proof of this statement:
  • Rome had the system of actual democracy, where both the rich and the poor had an equally important vote.
  • Rome founded the base for Capitalism
  • Roman armies used the first such great and disciplined formations in all of Europe(ex. The testudo)
I have no doubt the second statement must have raised a few eyebrows. But, this is as true as the statement that the Saxons came from the same region as the Vikings (The Saxons were Scandinavians!). If a common immigrant had left a record in the form of a diary, it could have been a lot like this: 28 June, 189 Dear Diary, Today I witnessed the most amazing things! I must preserve these writings so that men from future generation can imagine the Glory of this most incredible Era! I shall be a guide through this most magnificent system. The gate is well guarded, and it takes a nominal fee to go inside. The baths are a great place to be! I walked to this great big door, with guards at the gate. Those guards are big, burly men, with the gladius of a legionary. I pay the fee and walk in. I see a huge building that seems to touch the sky! I walk in, and the guard asks me to change my clothes. I walk out wearing great big robe. I feel like I am wearing a Kimono! (OK, nobody ever compared these two in those days, however, we do have to make sacrifices dont we? Especially in the name of the crusade to make people discover the actual history) find many signs written in Latin, steam coming out of some, and I heard the speaking of lots and lots of people. I recognize some voices. I am hearing the voices of the great senators! I am unable to walk in, try as I may, paranoia halts me. I somehow, for all I have ever known; take baby steps into a warm room. I walk in, and see a group of poor men seated close to the senators! This boosts me into a confidence I have never known, and I join the group. 10 minutes later, I start to feel bored and my body starts to grow stiff. I feel that I need some exercise, and I see a humongous room with men of all ages and nationalities exercising! I now walk up to the room, hang my clothes (in those days, people would exercise naked. The very word Gymnasium, is Greek for: School of naked exercise), place my purse, and start to exercise, just a basic warm-up. 5 minutes later, I start to lift weights, when I hear a big man say I can lift an amount so heavy you can never lift! Lift, you can't even imagine it! My other ear hears a slight whisper and a giggle after it hears another sentence- I can lift a hundred sestertiis! I turn I head around and see a small, frail man quietly grab the purse of the Big Burly Ursis, or Big Burly Bear. Now I turn to see I own purse. It is safe, Thank god! However, another man, tall, well-built, half-starved, dirty, has a look in his eyes that reminds I of the look in my eyes when I stole my friends marbles. That was how I became the marble-game champion of my tribe. Now I really get paranoid and wear my clothes, and pick up my purse. In my paranoia, I failed to notice that the pain in my body has built up to a great magnitude, and that I need a massage. I hear a big slap and a scream of a man who obviously was on the business end of the slap. I walk in expecting to see a big melodrama with two angry men fighting each other. Perhaps we might have a nice little non-brutal fight. Instead, I find myself in the massage room, and that the slaps are how the slaves are massaging the Romans! I observe that every slave dips his hands in a big bowl full of oil, their hands dripping with it, and they slap their hands on the area of the body they are currently massaging, and after the loud slap and the resulting red skin, the oil that spread after the slap is rubbed and is soaked by the skin. I have always been an adventurous person, and I decide to get a massage. 40 minutes later. I feel proud that I tried it, but my back, re-coloured, has a thing or two to say about that. Now, the oil is fresh on my skin, and I feel that I should give it some time before washing off. I look for a way to pass the time, when I stumble upon a library! Even though I have always been poor, I can read both Latin and Greek. I find Latin texts there, which I would expect to find in an area so close to Rome. After reading all of those great big Latin texts, I really wanted to read The Iliad by Homer but unfortunately, this great text has a translation, but the library does not contain the translation. I walk out, dejected, when I see a Sign written in Greek that says-Library! I walk inside and do read the great tale of war and ingenuity. Now, I am ready to cleanse off. As I walk inside and am about to dive into the cold water, a man tells I that this is the incorrect way to cleanse. He tells me the procedure. I then walk across the hall, and walk in a room full of warm water. The water has made the room warm as well, and now I sit here for ten minutes or so, before I walk to the room a bit warmer than that. In the room that seems to be hotter than the sun, I meet a Senator! Oh, lord, had this catastrophe not have fallen unto me! Lets hope I do not make him angry, for he may fly into a rage and devise and sentence a number of executions for me, which make crucifixion the most merciful of all!   (Please do pray for me) To be continued ...       Picture Credits: www.wings.buffalo.eu and www.historynotes.info"
