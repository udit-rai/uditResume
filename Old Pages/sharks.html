CITES- Savior of Sharks

Published 20 Apr 2013

"      CITES or the Convention on International Trade of Endangered Species has done it again! They have successfully added another five species of Sharks into the convention demanding the restriction, if not a replete ban, and this great and truly historic move has brought a fluorescent time in the history of marine wildlife. CITES has done something truly historic: giving life to another 5 species from their would-be exterminator’s: us. Truly, these great connoisseurs have saved the world from extermination by saving a crucial link of the food web that leads inexorably to us. One must appreciate this fantastic move. Already having established a strong foothold on the politics surrounding flora and fauna, they have delivered the gift of life not to one organism, but to entire species, again and again, riding like a knight on a silver horse dressed in shining armor. CITES had already saved multiple species of sharks, and, now, another 5 are protected.

    The Sharks that have been gifted with this great present of life are:
  1. The Oceanic White-Tip
  2. The Scalloped Hammerhead shark
  3. The Great Hammerhead
  4. The Smooth Hammerhead, and
  5. The Porbeagle.

This most amazing move has done justice to the effort of those who have spent their lives trying to educate people that sharks are living, feeling beings, and removing the dangerous paradigm that sharks are nothing but “Eating Machines” and mindless monsters and has also brought a complete makeover in the history of Human Conflict with Nature, the provider of all.
5 species of Sharks are now protected by law, and since they are unable to smile, let us do it for them.
This is my small contribution to spread awareness about the True Emperors of the Sea-
Udit Rai: Author of Forthcoming book: “Sharks- The emperors of the sea”.   Picture Credits: www.frenchtribune.com    "
