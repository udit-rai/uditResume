The Shark Manifesto

Published 27 Aug 2013

Welcome Back! As you may recall from my earlier blog on CITES, the conservation of sharks is taking place and more and more countries are banning the fishing of sharks. On the surface it appears that war is over and just a single battle more is to be fought, that too, one that is insanely one-sided (towards the conservationists). However, unfortunately, that is nothing but an illusion, a mirage. While the U.S. government has taken an excellent step by banning shark fishing and I salute them for it, but the American government’s jurisdiction doesn’t cover entire oceans. Actually not even one as I know it. In the big blue sea, in their own habitat, sharks are still being slaughtered in thousands every day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. But, today’s blog in not to worry about this awful situation, rather it is to share that steps are constantly being taken to alleviate the situation. And, my favorite Discovery has been taking huge steps. The step I am talking about is just great. Actually, way back in 1985, Discovery announced its annual “Shark Week”, a week-long “Shark Manifesto”, bringing the truth about these magnificent creatures to the masses. It has been doing a monumental job in making constant efforts to destroy what I like to call the “Jaws Syndrome”, a perception that has been launched by Jaws, a film directed by Steven Spielberg. Even the producer of the film, David Brown, later regretted producing the film, even though the movie earned a mind-boggling $470 million worldwide way back in 1970s! Why? Because the effects of the movie were astonishing. It formed a completely new meaning to the word “Shark”, so much that even though David Brown spent the rest of his life trying to save sharks from the jaws Image, but couldn’t do much. So, if you missed the “Shark Week” this year, be sure to catch it next time!! Till my next blog on sharks, enjoy some shark facts and some shark movies here. Udit"
