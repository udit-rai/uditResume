Do we really care about fellow human beings?

Published 06 Feb 2014

Greetings!
This blog is upon a very serious point, so I will cut straight to it.
Corruption. We hear a lot about it nowadays.  High profile politician was caught Crores of rupees have been found in the basement of... and more recently, The AAP has released a list of so called corrupt ministers who they will campaign against. But corruption has another meaning. Merriam Webster defines it as: impairment of integrity, virtue, or moral principle or depravity. Not a nice word to associated with. Unfortunately, many of us shall have to be described with this word.
So, have you ever been stuck in a traffic jam? Yes, of course, is that even a question?! That means that you have seen all those cars, that honking. And very frequently, you have to keep braking, often quite suddenly. This does release a lot of pressure. And as I said I was going to get to the point, this is the point:    
 The Times of India reports: A 21-year-old construction worker injured in a road accident died in a hospital after the ambulance that was taking him to a hospital was stuck in traffic for more than 30 minutes at Red Hills toll plaza on Sunday.   The ambulance that was rushing the injured to Stanley Medical College and Hospital got held at the end of a long queue of vehicles at an under-construction toll plaza between Red Hills and Karanodai. After more than 30 minutes, traffic cleared for the ambulance to pass. Rajesh was admitted to the hospital but he died at 5.30pm. When news of his death spread, residents and relatives blocked the road near the toll plaza. without realizing that this blocking of the roads may lead to another death because of delayed arrival at the Hospital. And it is not just us that face this problem. An extract from the Beijing Cream: This should be everyones fear about living in a city with such crippling traffic. In Beijing on Friday, a woman in her 50s died in the back of an ambulance because it was stuck in a gridlock. How bad was it? According to the doctor, Wang Yuzhu, as reported by Sina: The scene of the accident is no more than 3 kilometers from the hospital, yet it took us a good 40 minutes to arrive there! Barely any cars made way for our ambulance! So woeful! Wang wrote on Weibo. That is horrific. The ambulance driver, Zhang Shaojun, said about half of drivers on the road typically never give way for his vehicle.
In Taiwan earlier this year, a somewhat similar thing happened where cars didnt give way for an emergency vehicle carrying a victim who died en route. But Im not sure if we can chalk up what happened in Beijing to callousness. Ambulance: Life is at stake, please make way! Car: I want to make way, but I cant! Many people have slipped into comas, or even died, because the people in front of  them were too lazy to move. Many families have been ruined, just because the people in front were too scared to move for a person fighting death. What are they scared of? Losing their place in the line. In todays busy lives, we have forgotten the basics of humanity. We complain and moan about the children in Sierra Leone over tea, when the time for action comes, many of us are far too lazy or scared of being late to not interfere with a fellow human being fighting for his life.
SO, please ask yourself, do YOU give way to Ambulance? And if not, then please make a promise that YOU will from this day give way to the ambulance so you dont become the cause of someones death! ================================================================ picture credits: naziaftikhar.wordpress.com"
