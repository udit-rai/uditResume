Uttarakhand- Short Of The Apocalypse

Published 08 Jul 2013

Hello Everybody! Regret the long pause between blogs. I was out of station, enjoying the summer holidays. Well, theres only one of those in the whole year. Without internet connectivity, I was stranded. But, today, there are people who are not stranded from the internet, but from the rest of the world. Thats right, theyre trapped. Many of you may have recognized these details as the condition of the state of Uttarakhand, India. The people of this state are ravaged, almost annihilated by monster floods and the consequences of those. Many are trapped in caves, and tens of thousands are missing. Their worlds have turned upside down, turned on their head, and those are the lucky ones. For the rest, its simply torn apart. And those are the survivors. Many people have leaped from the terrace of the office buildings into the water to try to swim their way out. Some have accomplished this heroic act, the others lie with God.  So many of these people have perished, so many fatherless children cry, so many parents who have lost their children lie devastated. Mr. Manish Goel, a senior advocate in the Supreme Court, is stranded along with another unfortunate 3000 in Gangotri. This is his story as shared by NDTV: No food, no water, no gas, no electricity, no fuel - and absolutely no help. I and 500 others are stranded in Gangotri and have no idea how we are going to get out. We are staying at an ashram here, which has been feeding nearly 3000 people - almost the entire town - every day. But now, the ashram too has run out of supplies. The wood is wet, and we cannot even light up a stove. There is only one doctor attending to thousands of people in the area. Worse, we have run out of medicines. More and more people are falling sick. With no food or water, and no signs of help, the situation is reaching a tipping point. People are getting impatient. Children are crying all the time. Arguments and fights are breaking out. Locals are now saying they have the right to be rescued first when the help comes. Everybody is claiming they should be the first to get out of here. Frustration, anger and fear is taking over. It is getting ugly.  We are sitting on a ticking time bomb. Media says around 14,000 people are still missing and 60,000 still stranded. As for the missing, only god can help. As for the stranded, remember, it is difficult to keep track of those who have or are, because of the overwhelming conditions, turned or turning from stranded people into stranded bodies every single day. The focus of rescue operations right now is at Gaurikund, the area most affected. More than 5000 people are still trapped. The good news is that those of Kedarnath, the entire population, almost all of them have been rescued. Another 17 bodies have been found. The famous temple, a part of the char-dham yatra has been completely submerged. 8,500 soldiers have joined the rescue efforts. The Indian air force has commissioned an MI-26, the largest chopper in the world, to transport aviation fuel, critical for the 45 choppers already in service. So many are still trapped. If they do not get timely help, it will only result in their doom. So the appeal to you; please help. The Rotary Club of New Delhi(A part of Rotary International, world's biggest NGO), quick to act, has shone a ray of lightness in the pitch dark. This club is accepting donations starting from 5,000 rupees for a shelter kit.  A shelter kit means life. Your wallet may be left little the lighter, but is carrying a fatter wallet greater than the lives of theirs? Please donate, or they may perish.  I am a student of grade VIII and am doing my bit by sending the money for a shelter kit. I appeal to each one of my readers to please help, and make your cheques, your invaluable life-saving donations in favor of : Rotary New Delhi Service Foundation Society  and send to Rtn. Gaurav Bhargav Treasurer Rotary Club of New Delhi 12, Jangpura Road Bhogal New Delhi-14 The money will be used for purchasing shelter kits for those affected. The pictures on the given link are very disturbing, but they show the near-apocalypse condition of the state. I again appeal to you, to please help those affected. Photo & Information Credit: NDTV"
