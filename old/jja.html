The new Juvenile Justice Act:Crime against Children?

Published 29 Dec 2015

Greetings! As many of you have heard, a new legislation has been passed by the Rajya Sabha: The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection) of Children Act, 2015. Once the President signs his approval, the bill will become law. However, many experts have pointed out that one of the most important points of this bill (and the focus of this blog), the decision to lower the age of juveniles from 18 to 16 in case of heinous crimes is anything but care and protection of children. One of the major reasons of the passing of this bill was the public outrage on the release of the juvenile convicted in the December 16 rape and murder case. A quick glance at this case: On December 16, 2012, a 23 year old female physiotherapy student (Nirbhaya) boarded a bus with a male friend; they were returning from a movie. It was not later than 9:30 PM. There were six other men on the bus, among them a juvenile. One of them approached the man and asked him what he was doing with a girl at that time of the night. We all know India has traditionally had a highly patriarchal culture. It exists to a regrettably great extent, and though we as a nation are emerging from its overbearing shadow, much of India is still bound by the shackles of this medieval mindset. Returning to the case; the male friend lost his temper after hearing such a question, and during a heated exchange; slapped the man who asked the question. This man, heavily drunk, began beating the couple with a rod. The others joined him and some of them dragged the woman to the rear of the bus; raping her and assaulting her with a rod-like object. 5 of the men (adults) were sentenced to death; however the juvenile was sentenced to the maximum allowed sentence to a juvenile - 3 years in a correctional facility. He has been released, and this, as can be imagined, infuriated many and led to an outburst of public anger. Social media was flooded with tirades and open letters, and many jokes, such as- Man names unborn baby justice so he can proclaim justice was delivered in India. But the effect was not limited to satire on social media. The parents of the victim were utterly devastated, saying Crime has won. And the Rajya Sabha passed the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection) of Children Act, 2015. In doing so, the Rajya Sabha has veered away from history, as since the Children Act of 1960, the Parliament has focused on care and protection of children, and a focus to mend them (rehabilitation), rather than punishment. This raises a few questions: Do you think that Rajya Sabha was rather forced to pass this bill; by the masses of people fighting to keep him behind bars? Do you think the reason this was not done till December 22nd is because RS had not deliberated on this bill? Would you agree that this was not delayed on purpose? I believe it is safe to assume no party would gain much by not deliberating on this bill. Dont you think any party that would have pushed the passing of this bill would have improved its public image? And so, do you think the bill has been passed in haste? I am asking the above questions because many experts are raising their voices of the ills that this law may bring. Many among them are voices from the Justice Verma committee; the very committee tasked with reforming and invigorating anti-rape law. These voices have warned that such legislation puts all children across all classes at risk. Apparently, this is because India has a pressing issue of abuse of pro-women laws; particularly false rape cases. For example, a study of the 586 rape cases decided in the Delhi district courts found 53.2% of them to be of a dubious nature. Of the cases fully tried, over 40% dealt with consensual sex, with the girls parents filing a case of rape afterwards. Another 25% dealt with breach of promise to marry. If the victim refused to file such a false case, then, the complainants have disclosed that they have had to beatings, confinement, threats, being forced to undergo medical examinations, being forced to undergo abortions; from their own family. A famous lawyer and activist, Seema Mishra, claims- Once the girl is sent back to her parents, there is tremendous pressure on her and she often changes her stance. If the girl is not sent to a shelter once she is picked up by the police, and instead sent home, you can be sure they will brainwash her and she will change her statement in court. Also, Mr. Sanjay Hegde, a senior Supreme Court advocate, raises many points of injustices apparent in the near future due to this law. He explains that a majority of juvenile crime is non-violent crime, and most children involved in such cases come from extremely poor backgrounds and/or are runaways from hunger and abuse at home. A variety of crimes, many of them non-violent, can lead to sentencing for 7 years or more, classifying them as heinous crimes; qualifying them as adults. Among them are crimes such as: sedition and forgery; clearly non-violent acts. This has the result that, he says- Under the new law, a stone-pelting teenager in Kashmir or a teenage purveyor of counterfeit currency from Kanyakumari is likely to be treated as an adult criminal. Many experts have called this law a crime against children.

On the flipside, let us look at convicted rapists (and other convicts who qualify as adults under the new law). The human mind is not matured until after 20 years of age, and it may even take longer; as scientific research has revealed. . And, science says, rightly so. As per a 2007 study done by the NIMH (National Institute of Mental Health), US, the brain is not fully matured until well above 20 years of age. This study had Dr. Jay Geidd, a child and adolescence psychiatrist follow the MRI scans of around 1,000 healthy children from the age pool of 3-18. In a 2005 paper- Adolescence, brain development and legal culpability, he was quoted Part of the brain that is helping organization, planning and strategizing is not done being built yet Its sort of unfair to expect [adolescents] to have adult levels of organizational skills or decision-making before the brain is being built. Another study has established that under conditions of chronic and severe stress in rats, the prefrontal cortex can shrink by up to 40 per cent resulting in brain cells in this area losing their capacity to process information properly. Thus, the parts of the brain that are crucial for processing information about specific events, and making careful decisions based on them such as controlling high-risk behaviour are severely compromised. Simultaneously, the same stress pushes the amygdala, the emotional hub of the brain that is involved in fear, anxiety and aggression, in the opposite direction by making its neurons grow bigger and stronger. How is this relevant? Well, among many points in Mr. Sanjay Hegdes article, a point was raised that most juvenile delinquents come from abusive and dysfunctional homes; causing the aforementioned effects. Putting such people among hardened criminals only hardens and strengthens the devil within them, as people in such an age are far more vulnerable to bad influences. Various studies have shown that juveniles placed among hardened criminals are far more prone to recidivism than compared to those who are not. Neuroscience also says juvenile delinquents are more capable of change given the fact that their brains are still learning. Honest efforts made towards rehabilitation including visits by a mental health professional three-four times a month will have a significant positive impact on them. The following can be seen as proving this point- Leslee Udwin, the director of Indias Daughter says the juvenile is not a monster. She says that among all the rapists she interviewed, the juvenile was the only one to feel remorse. The top people of his correctional facility, say that he has improved significantly, turning from illiterate to literate, a good cook and has learned tailoring. They say that he personally supervises the food prepared during festivals, and especially during Iftaar. Let me clarify, I am strongly opposed to his act and all acts against women with even a fraction of such barbarity. All I wish to say through the above example is that juveniles are very prone to influences; good influences can even show its impact on doers of horrific crimes such as the juvenile, and so can bad influences. In the end, we are a democracy. The people are the most important; everything is of theirs, by them and for them. It is your wish, the peoples wish that will be fulfilled. All I attempted to do through this blog was to inform you that there is another side to this issue. I hope you shall agree that it is best to look before we jump; and we as a nation; have jumped quite deep in the form of this bill. I hope I have been able to portray the other side of this debate.
