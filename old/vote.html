The Messiah: Your Vote!

Published 22 Apr 2014

Welcome, just finished my final exams and am back. I realize I am more than a bit late, but this is the time for action, and a delay is much better than no action. So, we are in April 2014, and it’s election season in India. Everybody is after the votes of the masses, and thus the environment has changed significantly. The ads and news across all mediums have changed from other products to self-glory or the criticism of a rival parties’ way of administration, the sounds of traffic are replaced by the sounds of “So and So ko vote dain (Give your vote to so and so)” 4 or 5 times on average in a 15-minute journey. We, the general public, are made to feel special, instead of how it “normally” happens, where we are the subjects and the politicians are the kings.  Why is this? Because of the power of the vote. It is the most powerful weapon we as a democracy can wield. However, it is so frequently abused, in so many ways! Why?! Somebody can abuse the vote by voting on a caste basis, by voting for a gangster because of fear, and, by simply not voting! How can we expect change if we do not vote for the person who will bring change? Democracy has been around ever since Ancient Athens, and this whole form of government and power distribution is based on one event: Election! Election is when citizens show up to vote for the person they wish to represent and lead them. How can we expect a better democracy if we don’t fulfill our duty better!

Let’s understand that if we do not vote, we are working against ourselves, our future and our motherland. This problem is also seen in the UK. Many of the youth do not vote. Many wise people have taken the initiative, to politically enlighten the public at large, by various means, such as holding workshops like “Bite the Ballot”, to help such people realize the power behind EVERY SINGLE VOTE. The Times of India through their surveys have revealed a few of the reasons why students don’t vote. Here are a couple of reasons: A headline on the Delhi Times on March 31st 2014: “Elections not-so-cool this time around, says Delhi University”.  The article reveals that some DU students now place the upcoming elections lower than assignments and “dealing with hangovers” on their priorities. If we closely analyze the above reason, we realize that this article does not only reveal the mindset of some of the students, but of many of the youth all over India.  And this mindset is VERY concerning. I am nobody to preach, but if we don’t vote- We are simply damning ourselves. Why: The fact that this interviewed youth has placed voting beneath petty things indicates that they will not research before casting and that is the most insulting and stupid way to abuse the power of the vote. The vote is something our forefathers hanged for, smiling. The vote is what Emily Davison (The suffragette) sacrificed herself for. Insulting the vote means insulting Father of the Nation- Bapu, Mr. Bhagat Singh, Mr. Rajguru, Mr Sukhdev Singh Thapar, who accepted death for the freedom to vote, and so many freedom fighters who fought the English until death. It is insulting every single person who renounced the luxury and the stability of life, gave up their awards and titles, and came onto the streets, happily being beaten with sticks by the same men who used to salute them. Each single vote that is being wasted has cost so much blood that the very sands of time could not fully absorb. This abuse of the vote, this unwise voting is the same as convicting yourself and your family to an unstable life full of grief, sorrow and mental torture, all because when you were young you did not vote and some other unenlightened person voted for someone without understanding the impact of his/her own vote and selected the candidate unwisely. The whole country will have to pay if you do not vote wisely. Another reason reported by Times of India in its 31st March edition “I don't want to be a part of such a corrupt and filthy system because we have grown up watching what happens in our society. For example, it's a reality that the roads and street lights are repaired only before an impending election. No one even bothers to look at them for the rest of the year. What is the purpose of facilitating our so-called leaders by voting them when in return we don't even get the basic amenities? Gurgaon has no water or electricity or roads, what has any politician or leader nominating themselves for elections done this year?” This second reason is actually the very reason to vote! Why: Our vote has the power to kick such people out of power and appoint those who are actually public servants to power. If the right and the power that comes along with it were not given to the common man, then gangsters could be ruling the country.  Just imagine what would happen if each and every one of us voted wisely! If above is not enough, read the following to know the power of ONE VOTE:
  1. By one vote in 1875, France chose a republican rather than a monarchical form of government.
  2. One vote cost King Charles I of England his head in 1649.
  3. Women in America got the right to vote in 1920 by a single deciding vote.
If a single vote has the power to change the tides of time, we need to but imagine the power it has over India! Credits: Online Candidate.Com Times of India BBC News  "
